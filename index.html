Contextual Video Assistance for Cross-Application Creative Work

RePlay: Contextually Presenting Learning Videos Across Software Applications

Complex activities often require people to work across multiple software applications, each with its own set of conventions, tools, and features. To make it easier for people to find relevant help while they work, the RePlay system introduces an application-independent approach for contextually presenting video learning resources. RePlay uses accessibility APIs to gather context about the user’s activity. It leverages an existing search engine to present relevant videos and highlights key segments within them using video captions. A week-long field study (n=7) and a lab study (n=24) showed that contextual assistance helps people spend less time away from their task than web video search and replaces current video navigation strategies.

Publications:

RePlay: Contextually Presenting Learning Videos Across Software Applications. Proceedings of CHI '19. Honorable Mention.

Software videos: Rich content and learning potential, but a challenge for sensemaking. CHI '18 Sensemaking Workshop. (Workshop Paper)

Creative Livestreaming

Creative Livestreaming

Image sources: top, middle, bottom

Many artists livestream their creative process, allowing viewers to learn and be inspired from the decisions – and mistakes – they make along the way. To understand current practices and challenges in creative livestream communities, we have conducted a content analysis of livestream archives, interviews with streamers and viewers, and online surveys with viewers. Most recently, we have developed a semi-automatic approach for generating table of contents for creative live stream archives to make them easier to navigate and browse.

Interactive Guidance Techniques for Creative Feedback

CritiqueKit: Interactive Guidance Techniques for Improving Creative Feedback

Good feedback is critical to creativity and learning, yet rare. Many people do not know how to actually provide effective feedback. There is increasing demand for quality feedback – and thus feedback givers – in learning and professional settings. The CritiqueKit system presents two interactive techniques – reusable suggestions and adaptive guidance – that improve novice feedback on creative work. Two real-world deployment studies and two controlled experiments with CritiqueKit found that adaptively-presented suggestions improve the quality of feedback from novice reviewers.

Publications:

Interactive Guidance Techniques for Improving Creative Feedback. Proceedings of CHI '18. Honorable Mention.

CritiqueKit: A Mixed-Initiative, Real-Time Interface For Improving Feedback. UIST ’17. (Demo, Poster)

Collaborators:

Tricia Ngoon, Ariel Weingarten, Mira Dontcheva, Scott Klemmer.



Task Distribution for Collocated Workers

WeBuild: Automatic Assembly Task Distribution for Collocated Workers 



Physical construction and assembly tasks are often carried out by groups of collocated workers, and they can be difficult to coordinate. Group members must spend time deciding how to split up the task, how to assign subtasks to each other, and in what order subtasks should be completed. WeBuild is a task distribution system that dynamically assigns subtasks to workers in a group, taking into account factors such as the dependencies between subtasks and the skills of each group member. Each worker views personalized step-by-step instructions on a mobile phone, while a dashboard visualizes the entire process.

Publication:

WeBuild: Automatically Distributing Assembly Tasks Among Collocated Workers to Improve Coordination. Proceedings of CHI '17.

Collaborators:

Tovi Grossman, George Fitzmaurice.



Action Suggestions for Complex Software

DiscoverySpace: Suggesting Actions in Complex Software

Complex software offers power for experts, yet overwhelms new users. Novices often do not know how to execute tasks, what they want to achieve, or even what is possible. To address this, we introduce the DiscoverySpace interface for executable action suggestions. DiscoverySpace is a prototype extension panel for Adobe Photoshop that suggests task-level action macros to apply to photographs based on visual features. DiscoverySpace harvests these one-click actions from the online Photoshop user community. A between-subjects study indicated that action suggestions may help novices maintain confidence, accomplish tasks, and discover features. This work demonstrates how interfaces can leverage user-generated content to help novices navigate complex software.

Publications:

DiscoverySpace: Suggesting Actions in Complex Software. Proceedings of DIS ’16.

DiscoverySpace: Crowdsourced Suggestions Onboard Novices in Complex Software. CSCW ’16 Companion. (Extended Abstract) -- Demo

Collaborators:

Mira Dontcheva, Holger Winnemöller, Sheryl Ehrlich, Scott Klemmer.



Framing Peer Feedback

Framing Feedback 

Framing of a peer review task can significantly impact reviews. In this 3-part study, subtle feature changes in rubrics, task structure, and artifact representation resulted in reviews that were significantly different in both the quality and focus of reviewer feedback.

As part of this project, I built a website to host our first study, where graduate school applicants were invited to submit their application essay and give feedback to each other using PeerStudio.

See the Framing Feedback Project Website.

Publications:

Framing Feedback: Choosing Review Environment Features that Support High Quality Peer Assessment. Proceedings of CHI ’16.

Do numeric ratings impact peer reviewers? Learning at Scale, Vancouver, BC. (Extended Abstract) -- Poster

Collaborators:

Catherine Hicks, Vineet Pandey, Purvi Desai, Scott Klemmer.

Real-time Editing of Material Properties

Real-time Editing of Material Properties (Winter 2015)

The ability to interactively edit materials in a rendered scene is important for graphic artists and designers. Rather than adjusting the values of parameters for an analytic BRDF (Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function) model, a more intuitive method would be to paint edits directly on the rendered image and interactively see results. For my project with Ravi Ramamoorthi, I explored the challenges and possibilities for building such an intuitive BRDF editing system.

Read my final report here.
