Real-time Editing of Material Properties

Real-time Editing of Material Properties (Winter 2015)

The ability to interactively edit materials in a rendered scene is important for graphic artists and designers. Rather than adjusting the values of parameters for an analytic BRDF (Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function) model, a more intuitive method would be to paint edits directly on the rendered image and interactively see results. For my project with Ravi Ramamoorthi, I explored the challenges and possibilities for building such an intuitive BRDF editing system.

Read my final report here.

Communication with Locked-in Syndrome

Communication with Locked-in Syndrome (Fall 2014)

As our project for CSE218 (Advanced Topics in Software Engineering: Ubiquitous Computing), my group built a system called SpeedyType for people who suffer from locked in syndrome to type messages using only their eyes. Our system also included a piano keyboard for playing music using the eyes. See a video of our demo here.

Primal-dual Coding

Exploring Extensions of Primal-dual Coding (2013-2014)

I worked as a research assistant for Kyros Kutulakos in the Dynamic Graphics Project lab at the University of Toronto. I studied BRDF acquisition and visual texture analysis, and extended an existing “primal-dual coding” camera system to isolate light transport based on the direction and distance of travel.
